eTIMS Implementation Guide for Small Businesses

By Tax Home Kenya Limited

The Electronic Tax Invoice Management System (eTIMS) has become mandatory for all businesses in Kenya. If you're a small business owner and haven't implemented eTIMS yet, this comprehensive guide will walk you through the process, requirements, and best practices to ensure smooth implementation.

What is eTIMS?

eTIMS is a digital system introduced by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) to manage tax invoices electronically. It requires businesses to transmit invoice data in real-time to the KRA's systems, improving tax compliance and reducing tax evasion. The system integrates with your Point of Sale (POS) system or invoicing software to automatically capture and transmit invoice information.

Who Needs to Register for eTIMS?

All persons engaged in business are required to register.The law requires that for any person to claim their business expense, the expense must be supported by an electronic tax invoice. Therefore, all persons engaged in business are required to issue electronic tax invoices, whether registered for VAT or not (non-VAT taxpayers). .The solutions available are:-

  • Online Portal- Tailored for taxpayers in the service sector exclusively, where no goods are supplied
  • eTIMS Client – A downloadable software designed for taxpayers dealing in goods or both goods and services. The software supports multiple branches and pay points/cashier tills
  • Virtual Sales Control Unit (VSCU) – This solution enables seamless system-to-system integration between the taxpayer’s invoicing/ERP system and eTIMS, catering to taxpayers with extensive transactions or bulk invoicing
  • Online Sales Control Unit (OSCU) – This solution also facilitates system-to-system integration between the taxpayer’s invoicing/ERP system and eTIMS. It is ideal for taxpayers using an online invoicing system

eTIMS can be installed on either of the following devices: Windows based computers & laptops. Android smart phones, tablets & Personal Digital Assistant (PDA) devices

Step-by-Step eTIMS Implementation Process

Step 1: Verify Your Registration Status

Before implementing eTIMS, confirm whether your business is required to register:

  • Log into your KRA iTax account
  • Check your PIN registration details
  • Review any notifications from the KRA regarding eTIMS requirements
  • Contact the KRA if you're unsure about your status

Step 2: Choose an eTIMS-Compliant System

You'll need to select a Point of Sale (POS) system or invoicing software that is eTIMS-compliant. Many popular systems have been certified by the KRA:

  • Ensure the system is KRA-certified
  • Verify it meets all eTIMS technical specifications
  • Check that it integrates with your current business operations
  • Consider the cost and support available
💡 Tip: Many POS providers offer eTIMS integration packages. Compare options and choose one that best fits your business needs and budget.

Step 3: Register with eTIMS

Once you have a compliant system, register your business with eTIMS:

  • Access the eTIMS portal through the KRA website
  • Enter your business PIN and required information
  • Upload necessary documentation
  • Receive your eTIMS registration credentials

Step 4: Configure Your POS System

After registration, configure your POS system to connect with eTIMS:

  • Install the eTIMS integration software
  • Enter your eTIMS credentials in the system
  • Configure invoice settings and tax codes
  • Test the connection to ensure proper data transmission

Step 5: Train Your Staff

Ensure your team understands the new system:

  • Conduct training sessions on the new POS system
  • Explain the importance of eTIMS compliance
  • Provide clear procedures for daily operations
  • Establish a support system for technical issues

Step 6: Go Live and Monitor

Launch eTIMS operations and monitor performance:

  • Start with a trial period if possible
  • Monitor invoice transmission to the KRA
  • Check for any error messages or failed transmissions
  • Keep detailed records of all transactions

Key Requirements for eTIMS Compliance

Real-Time Invoice Transmission: All invoices must be transmitted to the KRA in real-time as they are issued. Delayed or batch transmission is not acceptable.

Accurate Data Entry: Ensure all invoice information is accurate, including customer details, item descriptions, quantities, and prices.

Proper Tax Coding: Use the correct tax codes for different products and services. Incorrect coding can result in compliance issues.

System Backup: Maintain backup systems in case of internet connectivity issues. eTIMS-compliant systems include offline functionality that syncs when connection is restored.

Record Retention: Keep all invoice records and eTIMS transmission reports for at least 5 years as required by law.

Common Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Internet Connectivity Issues

Solution: Ensure stable internet connection. Consider a backup internet provider or use mobile hotspot as a backup. eTIMS systems have offline capabilities that sync when connection is restored.

Challenge 2: System Integration Problems

Solution: Work with your POS provider's technical support team. Ensure your system is fully updated and certified for eTIMS.

Challenge 3: Staff Resistance to Change

Solution: Provide thorough training and ongoing support. Emphasize the benefits of eTIMS for compliance and business operations.

Challenge 4: Data Accuracy Concerns

Solution: Implement quality control procedures. Regularly audit invoice data to ensure accuracy.

Benefits of eTIMS Implementation

  • Improved Compliance: Automatic transmission ensures timely and accurate reporting to the KRA
  • Reduced Errors: Digital system minimizes manual data entry errors
  • Better Record Keeping: Centralized digital records are easier to maintain and audit
  • Tax Transparency: Clear audit trail for all transactions
  • Operational Efficiency: Streamlined invoicing and reporting processes

Timeline for Implementation

The KRA has provided specific timelines for different business categories. Check your notification letter for your specific deadline. Generally:

  • Phase 1 (Retail): Already mandatory
  • Phase 2 (Hospitality): Mandatory since 2024
  • Phase 3 (Other sectors): Ongoing expansion
